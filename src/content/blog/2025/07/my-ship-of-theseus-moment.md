So yeah, he is pretty popular for someone who has read basic Greek mythology. I do believe his ship is more famous. Probably because I am ignorant. Probably it is more famous. Can't say for sure. Don't know enough.It doesn't matter actually. Regardless of what is more famous, his ship is quite good thought experiment. Again, probably discussed to death, but its always intriguing.&nbsp;</p><p>So the thought experiment goes like this. A ship, any ship requires repairs. Let's say you keep repairing it and eventually you end up replacing every single part of it. So nothing is original anymore. Would you still call it the same ship?</p><p>&nbsp;Anyway, not here to entertain that thought. Or maybe I am, because I am about to write a love letter about my PC. It was the winters of 2020. January I think. I was contemplating about upgrading my laptop but decided against it. I did want to play games now that I was finally earning. So having a better machine was a no-brainer. But for some reason, I don't remember why, I decided to assemble my own gaming rig. However humble, since I really wasn't earning that much, I wanted one. And the best way to accomplish that was marrying the old guts that someone didn't need anymore with fresh new memory and looks (the cabinet). And that is exactly what I did. I scourged OLX for quality used parts. Even found a lot of them. I got great deals on the GPU, CPU and the motherboard. Decided not to take chance on second hand memory, so got a new RAM, hard drive, SSD, a power supply and that's pretty much what it takes to assemble a PC.</p><p>I will obviously never forget that trip to CP to some rich guy's house where he sold me his used motherboard and CPU. Or the trip to green line metro where I got my GPU. It did take me 4 hrs of travel but damn it was worth it.&nbsp;</p><p>And honestly, I was damn proud of myself. It was a good rig. For the budget, it was excellent. These past 5 years, I have gotten out so much use from it. It has helped me pass time, discover new things, experiment extensively. And of course, game. I have played so much FIFA on it, I can't count the hours. And no matter how much my arsenal of devices kept expanding, my PC is that one constant which I will never leave.&nbsp;</p><p>Or so I thought. It has recently given up on me. It is not POSTing. I went to a friend's home 1.5 hrs away just to make sure all the parts are working, and they are. So its essentially a motherboard problem. I now am entertaining the idea of replacing the whole thing, or just the motherboard at least. And as I thought about it, I felt sad. I don't want to leave this friend.</p><p>But then I thought more about it. Is it even the friend I once recognized?&nbsp; I have upgraded it twice already, replacing the CPU, GPU and RAM with better alternatives. So is it really the same? Sure all the other parts are original, but its not the same display port in which I once plugged the monitor. Heck, even the monitor is not the same. I have upgraded that too.&nbsp;</p><p>This situation felt too akin to Ship of Theseus. But is it? This motherboard I was feeling sad about, it isn't originally mine. It belonged to someone else. So in essence, my PC belongs to him too? And if that is the case, then why did I feel sad? why did it not matter when I replaced the previous parts? Maye because I knew that they are not really broken. They will be used by someone else. The spirit will live on.&nbsp;</p><p>Let's end it here. Now go watch Ship of Theseus. Yes that Amir Khan one. It's a good movie.&nbsp;</p>
