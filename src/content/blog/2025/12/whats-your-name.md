<div class="separator"><p style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"></p><div style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em; text-align: center;"><img class="sFlh5c FyHeAf iPVvYb" src="https://preview.redd.it/3vexh8w3nw271.jpg?width=640&amp;crop=smart&amp;auto=webp&amp;s=d831fc244318ca9a8487e42f66a3fb0d4a85a974" style="height: 537px; margin-bottom: 0px; margin-top: 0px; max-width: 640px; width: 302px;" /></div>When you meet a stranger, its the very first question after the greeting. Your names get exchanged, now you are no longer strangers. Names have powers. They are a crucial part of your entity. The world knows you by your name. The more your name is taken, the more you are remembered. It's sort of a parallel belief system. And that is quite visible when you think about the the gods. A more popular more powerful god is one whose name is taken by more people everyday. You also understand the influence of local deities this way. They are powerful but only in the vicinity of their core presence. Because the name is prevalent among the locals. To understand this concept better, I really hope you re watch Kantara or maybe read Bleach. Both of them deal with it excellently. Before Kantara hardly anyone apart from the "locals" knew Guliga. But that movie has spread the name everywhere. Now since more people are aware, more people know the name, more people believe in it.&nbsp;<br />Oh you're not the one to connect with gods? That's alright. You can easily translate this analogy to brands or companies. Yes the products and the quality matter, but in the end its the name that will prevail. Nuances will be forgotten. This I will leave to you as an assignment if you're interested. I couldn't care less if you feel otherwise.&nbsp;<p></p></div><p>Anyway, coming to the point. Why am I blabbering about names all of a sudden? Let me explain you the situation currently. I am sitting here in my house, my flatmates' parents are visiting. It's 9:50 am in the morning, its a relatively chilly and foggy day and I am mostly done with all the morning stuff I could think of. I have exercised (yes I did, fuck off), I have gone for a walk, I have spent time on twitter and youtube, I have turned on the geyser to take bath and most importantly I have made chai and shared it with my flatmates' parents who are visiting. I even have had a talk with my parents today. So yeah pretty complete morning.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>And here I am sitting on the sofa with his father sitting in front of me and I couldn't help but think about how I do not know what his name is. I met him yesterday, We had dinner together, went for a walk in the morning and yet I only refer to him as uncle. And yes while I agree that even if I knew his name, I would still call him uncle, it is still staggering how we overlook this dynamic. Maybe it is just a me problem but I don't think a lot of people my age actively seek out names of the people a bit older than us. And the weird part is that he knows my name, he knew it before he came to the flat. And yet somehow we (people like me) skip this part altogether. And would it be weird if I ask for his name? I am not entirely sure.<br /></p><p>It just tells you about how weirdly social conventions work. There are certain expectations. But there are also a lot of common things which people will agree are important but seem to overlook anyway. Like anyone would agree that asking for the name of person you just met is a normal thing to do. but with added context, suddenly its not important anymore. I will just refer to him as Gaurav's father in case I have to mention him to a third person (Gaurav included).&nbsp;</p><p>Ok it's 10 am now, need to go to office too. So I'll just end it right here. And yes, I don't know his mother's name also but its just that I interacted with his father more so i have written about him.&nbsp;<br /><br />Ok thanks bye!&nbsp;</p>
