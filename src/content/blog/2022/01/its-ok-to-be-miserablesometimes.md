At regular intervals you're bound to feel miserable. The degree of misery sure does vary a lot. And there are certainly differences in how one handles such phases. But for the most part, feeling miserable is not very ideal. You have that knot around your chest area, your shoulders are droopy, your posture isn't right, your head is down for the most part and you struggle to smile. While you physically struggle to perform simple tasks, your brain is simultaneously on overdrive. The thoughts race in your mind as if you're trying to solve one of the problems of the Millennium Prize. You think about all kinds of what could have beens, what ifs, and whys. It sucks.<p></p><p>Now this might not be the case with you, but personally I feel everyone will try and get out of this situation to the best of their ability. For my case, I try to distract myself, drown myself into endless playlists of YouTube videos, try and play video games till the depths of the night. And honestly, this distraction doesn't sound too bad but it feels as if you're grinding away at the things you like to do. But I continue to do it because I see no other path.</p><p>I'm not going to tell you to keep powering through, that it will get better. Because how would I know? I don't know what situation you're in, how miserable you're feeling. I am not aware of the circumstances that surround you. I am not going to compare it with a quicksand. I am not saying that the more you struggle, the more you'll get stuck in the quicksand and you just need to wait calmly for some external stimulus to rescue you from this horrible place. Because while that might be accurate, it might not feasible. Or if it is indeed feasible, it might be way too difficult. The wait just might kill you.</p><p>Instead, I want you to focus on the feeling itself. You're FEELING miserable. You're FEELING something. You're not feeling nothing. You're not a void. You have a reaction to the things happening around you. You're still functioning. You still care about things. Care about things that matter to you. Care about your family, friends, you career maybe, your dog, money, cheese, whatever. It is indeed a much better place than being nowhere. Enjoy the misery, focus on how it is affecting you. Understand it better. Maybe in the process of understanding, you'll find your branch to get out of the quicksand. Don't ignore it, embrace it.</p><p>Ultimately, time heals everything. Your misery will go away, hopefully. I like to believe there is something positive to what you've just experienced. And that would be resetting your references, calibrating your benchmarks. Benchmarks of happiness. If you've been at your low points for long, it becomes you base. Now you have nowhere to go but upwards. So enjoy your happy phase. Enjoy it to the fullest. The misery has made it much more sweeter.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>Pic unrelated.<br /></p>
