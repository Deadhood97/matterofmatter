Once upon a time, I created a kingdom. A fairly new kingdom, not unlike others. Had standard monarchs, the peasants, the bourgeoisie (if you would like it to have some). It had military as well, basically nothing out of the ordinary. The people of the kingdom, let's call them Narvols, were created along with the kingdom. So they didn't really know how to live and function in society. But the bigger kingdoms, Pilor and Milor, helped a ton in the development of the new kingdom. They gifted the kingdom specialized military units to train and prepare it for attacks of the unknown. They also made sure that the new kingdom was full of supplies and grains since the Narvols did not yet have any concept of farming.<br>
<br>
Narvols were showing extraordinary growth with the help they received. So much so that it even attracted a new family that goes by the name Flum. The Flums mixed with Narvols so well so fast. Pilorians and Milorians were happy to see this. Nobody knew however from where did the Flums come from. They were a small family, so nobody bothered much.<br>
<br>
As the years passed, Narvols were growing at an even faster rate. The grains provided by Pilorians and Milorians while adequate for Narvols were not enough to be shared by Flums as well. The reason was the enhanced physical activities of Narvols. They had discovered that sports and games helped in their development. They were catching on with the world right now. They could not spare the grain for Flums. As a result, the Flums vanished and Narvols continued to grow.<br>
<br>
Some more years passed, Narvols had reached the size of a normal kingdom. Their progress finally hit saturation. The Narvols were now capable of farming their own crops. They did not depend anymore on Pilorians and Milorians. The Narvols were indebted to them for all their support and teachings as an emerging kingdom should be. But now they had to fend for themselves, the period of handholding was over.<br>
<br>
Narvols were getting a hang for farming. They were becoming mighty good at it. They loved producing grain and since there were no other kingdoms to support, they had a surplus. They were invested so much in farming that they did not have time for sports and games. The surplus produce was no secret and just like that, the Flums were back. Again, nobody was aware of where they came from. They were little in numbers, the Narvols paid no attention to them.<br>
<br>
Little did the Narvols know, the Flums were a fan of surplus grain. They consumed all of it. They were growing now. Little by little their numbers were rising. Narvols had taken notice of it. But they pretended to ignore it since the Flums did not seem very harmful. The surplus was not surplus anymore. Narvols had to account for the Flums as well now. They kept on producing more grains. And the Flum numbers kept increasing. The other kingdoms had started to notice the growth of Flums in the kingdom. The Pilorians decided to warn Narvols of the hazards of high Flum populations. But the Milorians stopped them from doing so as they felt it was not their place to tell the Narvols the workings of their kingdom.<br>
<br>
The Narvols were not idiots, they were aware of the kind of disasters that the high Flum population brought. They had seen it other kingdoms. They had witnessed how lack of space had forced the Flum population in other kingdoms to come out on the streets, the same streets which were used for supplying grains. The situation had become so dire for Pilorians in past years that they had to construct several new roads for grain supply. Narvols were feeling guilty that the situation in their own kingdom had come to this.<br>
<br>
But Narvols were optimistic about the problem. They knew the solution. They had to burn the Flums. There was no other way of getting rid of them. They could control the extent of farming they did but they had gotten accustomed to a certain lifestyle now which was not easy to get rid of. And thus started the practice of burning Flums. At first, Narvols were excited. They were burning the Flums. But they got weary fast, for the process of burning Flums was exhausting. And it took very long to burn a single Flum.<br>
<br>
The Flums are still growing in the kingdom. Once a while the Narvols get motivated to burn the Flums again. But I believe it would really be better for them to just control the grain supply if they want the Flums to die on their own. Whatever, it's not my place to tell them. I'm just their God.</div>
