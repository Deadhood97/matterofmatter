Made of cotton, super light, airy and somewhat cute looking. They are perfect for the kind of weather I have to endure everyday. An since they are full length, they even provide me protection from the nasty mosquitoes. I did not buy them. My mom sewed them for me when some of the cloth was left over from another piece she was stitching. So basically they are free apart from the priceless motherly love of course which holds value for only me.&nbsp;<div><br /></div><div>As you can guess I wear them very frequently. They give me comfort. I kinda wish I could wear them anywhere. And yes I cannot wear them everywhere anytime. But why do I have to conform to what the society dictates, right? Well actually the society doesn't dictate what kind of pants you can/cannot wear but some of the rules are just unspoken.</div><div><br /></div><div>Example time! Like everyday, you get up, get ready for your commute to your workplace, you reach there, and the moment you sit at your desk,<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><br /></div> you look to your side. The person to your side is wearing the yellow white checked pajamas. Today is his first day, and apparently all he has is are multiple pairs of these same pajamas. He will wear them everyday. At first you think wtf. The moment you finish your thought, some other guy just went to the manager to check if this is really ok. The manager is puzzled as well. He has never seen this absurdity before in his life and yes office wear has been leaning more and more to casual clothing but this was just another level. The manager contacted HR and soon the new kid with checked pajamas receives communication about the dress code of the company. The boy is confused as well. He did not know this could happen, this was his first job after all (Yes, the boy is extremely ignorant. I mean who doesn't know what to wear in office, right?). The boy replies back to the HR mail with "But why?". The HR on the other side of the mail has no clue what to answer. He has no arguments but this is simply not acceptable. This is not professional behaviour according the company policy. And now the situation is not about what he is wearing, it has escalated to obeying authority or losing the job.&nbsp;</div><div><br /></div><div>And if you think the story is a bit exaggerated, just try and wear your pajamas to work for a day. And even if you don't get contacted by the HR, you sure as hell would feel extremely uncomfortable with a lone pajama in a sea of pants and jeans. So I guess it defeats the purpose of wearing those ever comfortable pajamas. And that for me is the ultimate L i.e. accomplishing the polar opposite effect from the intended one.&nbsp;</div><div><br /></div><div>I do not claim that no one can rock those pajamas anywhere anytime. Sure there'll be people who would get ready to go on a solo expedition to Sagarmatha wearing those comfy pair of trousers, but I would rather wear them at my home where I actually feel comfortable wearing them. Sometimes I do wish I should make a conscious effort to feel at ease whenever I'm wearing the pajamas no matter the situation but I would rather not.&nbsp;</div><div><br /></div><div>And what is the guarantee that there won't be a time when pajamas will be socially acceptable and then the pants will not suffer the same fate as the pajamas in the past. The transition period could definitely be more peaceful but once the people forget about the pajama life of past, it will be hell for the pants gang. The cycle will then continue. I hope some writer then refers to this piece and writes something similar but for pants so the society could witness the cruel circle of fate.</div><div><br /></div><div>What do you mean why am I giving so much thought to the simple ass pajamas today. I always have been (👦🔫).</div><div><br /></div>
